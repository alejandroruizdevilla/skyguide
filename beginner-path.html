Week 1: Getting Started

Foundation & Familiarization

Learning Objectives
  • Set up your simulator and basic controls
  • Understand cockpit layout and primary flight instruments
  • Master basic control inputs (pitch, roll, yaw)
  • Complete your first successful takeoff and landing

Daily Activities

Day 1-2: Setup & Settings
Install simulator, configure graphics, set up basic joystick/yoke controls, complete in-game tutorials
Day 3-4: Cockpit Familiarization
Learn the 6-pack instruments (airspeed, attitude, altitude, turn coordinator, heading, VSI). Practice reading instruments in different aircraft.
Day 5-7: First Flights
Start with Cessna 172 or similar trainer. Practice straight and level flight, gentle turns, climbs and descents. Attempt pattern work (traffic pattern circuits).

Week 2: Basic Flight Maneuvers

Building Core Skills

Learning Objectives
  • Execute standard rate turns accurately
  • Maintain altitude and airspeed in various configurations
  • Perform coordinated climbs and descents
  • Complete traffic pattern circuits consistently

Daily Activities

Day 8-10: Turns & Coordination
Practice 15¬∞, 30¬∞, and 45¬∞ banked turns. Learn to use rudder for coordinated flight. Try "turn to a heading" exercises.
Day 11-12: Climbs & Descents
Practice maintaining climb/descent rates. Learn power settings for different phases of flight. Try VY (best rate) and VX (best angle) climbs.
Day 13-14: Pattern Work
Fly complete traffic patterns. Practice crosswind, downwind, base, and final legs. Aim for 5-10 circuits per session with full-stop landings.

Week 3: Navigation Basics

Going Places

Learning Objectives
  • Read and interpret aeronautical charts
  • Navigate using VOR and GPS
  • Plan and fly a simple cross-country route
  • Understand basic radio communication procedures

Daily Activities

Day 15-17: Chart Reading
Study sectional charts. Learn symbols for airports, airspace, terrain. Practice finding landmarks. Use SkyVector or similar tools.
Day 18-19: VOR Navigation
Learn VOR principles. Practice intercepting and tracking radials. Fly VOR-to-VOR routes. Understand TO/FROM indicators.
Day 20-21: First Cross-Country
Plan a 50-100nm flight between two airports. Calculate heading, time, fuel. Fly the route using pilotage and dead reckoning.

Week 4: Putting It Together

Real-World Scenarios

Learning Objectives
  • Fly complete missions from planning to shutdown
  • Handle basic weather conditions
  • Use ATC (AI or human on VATSIM)
  • Develop consistent procedures and checklists

Daily Activities

Day 22-24: Weather Flying
Practice in various weather: light winds, crosswinds, reduced visibility, different cloud layers. Learn to read METARs and TAFs.
Day 25-27: ATC Communication
Use AI ATC or join VATSIM as Observer. Practice standard phraseology. Fly into controlled airports. Request flight following.
Day 28-30: Complete Flights
Plan and fly realistic scenarios: short hops between small airports, scenic flights, touch-and-go practice. Use checklists for every phase.

What's Next?

After completing your first 30 days, you'll have a solid foundation. Here are your next steps:

  • Start instrument training (IFR procedures and approaches)
  • Try more complex aircraft (twins, turboprops, jets)
  • Join a virtual airline or online network
  • Explore different types of flying (bush, aerobatics, gliding)
  • Consider studying for real-world pilot ratings
