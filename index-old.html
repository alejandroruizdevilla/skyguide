The Ultimate Guide to Flight Simulators

Welcome to the comprehensive flight simulator guide. Whether you're a beginner pilot or a seasoned veteran, this guide contains detailed information about the best flight simulators available in 2025.

Professional Flight Simulators

Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024

BEST GRAPHICS
DEVELOPER: Asobo Studio / Xbox Game Studios RELEASE: November 2024 PLATFORMS: PC (Windows), Xbox Series X|S, PS5 PRICE: $69.99 Standard / $99.99 Premium / Game Pass TYPE: Civilian aviation - General aviation to airliners
OVERVIEW:

The latest iteration of Microsoft's legendary flight simulator series. MSFS 2024 sets the gold standard for visual fidelity and real-world accuracy. Features the entire planet with satellite imagery, real-time weather, and living world with AI-generated traffic.

KEY FEATURES:
  • Photorealistic satellite-based scenery covering the entire Earth (2+ petabytes)
  • Real-time weather with live METAR data integration
  • 65+ highly detailed aircraft (from ultralights to A320neo)
  • Career mode with missions spanning bush flying, cargo ops, aerial firefighting
  • Full VR support with incredible immersion
  • Multiplayer with shared cockpit capability
  • Cloud streaming technology for reduced storage requirements
  • Massive add-on ecosystem with study-level aircraft available
TECHNICAL SPECS:
  • ENGINE: Proprietary Asobo engine with DirectX 12
  • MIN REQUIREMENTS: RTX 2060 / Ryzen 5 2600 / 16GB RAM
  • RECOMMENDED: RTX 4070 / Ryzen 7 5800X / 32GB RAM
  • STORAGE: 50GB base + optional streaming
  • INTERNET: Required for full features (offline mode available)
REALISM:
GRAPHICS:
BEGINNER FRIENDLY:
SYSTEM DEMANDS:
Best for: Sightseeing, VFR flying, casual to serious simulation. Perfect for experiencing flight over your hometown or traveling the world virtually.

X-Plane 12

BEST PHYSICS
DEVELOPER: Laminar Research RELEASE: November 2022 (actively updated) PLATFORMS: Windows, macOS, Linux PRICE: $59.99 Digital / Free demo available TYPE: Professional-grade civilian flight simulation
OVERVIEW:

X-Plane 12 is renowned for its physically accurate flight model using blade element theory. FAA-certified versions are used for actual pilot training. The go-to choice for serious simmers and student pilots who demand authentic aerodynamics.

KEY FEATURES:
  • Blade element theory flight model (each wing section calculated independently)
  • FAA-approved training device when used with certified hardware
  • Seasonal textures and improved weather modeling
  • 3D volumetric clouds and new lighting system
  • Advanced ATC system with realistic phraseology
  • Detailed aircraft systems including full IFR capabilities
  • Huge add-on community (FlightFactor, ToLiss, ZIBO 737)
  • Powerful built-in aircraft editor (Plane Maker)
  • VR support with excellent performance
TECHNICAL SPECS:
  • ENGINE: Custom X-Plane engine with Vulkan/Metal support
  • MIN REQUIREMENTS: GTX 1070 / i5-8400 / 16GB RAM
  • RECOMMENDED: RTX 3060 / Ryzen 7 5700X / 32GB RAM
  • STORAGE: 100GB+ with scenery
  • INTERNET: Optional (scenery downloads recommended)
REALISM:
GRAPHICS:
BEGINNER FRIENDLY:
SYSTEM DEMANDS:
Best for: Student pilots, IFR training, systems study, professional simulation. If you're training for a real pilot's license, this is your best bet.

Lockheed Martin Prepar3D v6

PROFESSIONAL
DEVELOPER: Lockheed Martin RELEASE: 2024 (v6.0) PLATFORMS: Windows only PRICE: $59.95 Academic / $199.95 Professional TYPE: Professional training platform (civilian use allowed)
OVERVIEW:

Based on the Microsoft ESP engine, Prepar3D is developed for professional training organizations but available for academic/personal use. Successor to FSX with modern graphics and systems. Used by airlines and military for actual crew training.

KEY FEATURES:
  • Compatible with many FSX add-ons (huge existing library)
  • Active Sky weather engine integration for ultra-realistic conditions
  • Professional-grade SDK for custom development
  • Multi-channel display support for simulator setups
  • Dynamic lighting with TrueSky technology
  • Study-level aircraft available (PMDG, Aerosoft, Majestic)
  • Excellent VR implementation
  • Professional user base provides quality add-ons
REALISM:
GRAPHICS:
BEGINNER FRIENDLY:
Best for: Professional training, airline procedure practice, study-level aircraft enthusiasts. Requires academic/professional qualification for purchase.

Aerofly FS 4

SMOOTH PERFORMANCE
DEVELOPER: IPACS RELEASE: 2023 PLATFORMS: Windows, macOS, Linux, iOS, Android, Nintendo Switch PRICE: $49.99 PC / $9.99 Mobile TYPE: Casual to mid-core flight simulation

Aerofly FS 4 excels at providing smooth, buttery performance even on mid-range systems. Features beautiful VR implementation and quick loading times. Perfect for pilots who want to jump in and fly without extensive setup.

Military & Combat Simulators

DCS World (Digital Combat Simulator)

BEST COMBAT SIM
DEVELOPER: Eagle Dynamics RELEASE: 2008 (continuously updated) PLATFORMS: Windows PC only PRICE: FREE base + $49.99-$79.99 per aircraft module TYPE: Hardcore combat flight simulation
OVERVIEW:

DCS World is THE definitive combat flight simulator. Each aircraft is a study-level module requiring dozens of hours to master. From Cold War jets to modern 4th-gen fighters, DCS offers unprecedented depth in weapons systems and tactics simulation.

KEY FEATURES:
  • Study-level aircraft (F-16C, F/A-18C, A-10C, F-14B, Mirage 2000C, etc.)
  • Full clickable cockpits with every switch functional
  • Advanced radar simulation and countermeasures
  • Realistic weapons employment (A-A and A-G)
  • Mission editor for creating complex scenarios
  • Dynamic campaign with combined arms
  • Multiplayer with dedicated servers and persistent wars
  • Free base includes Su-25T and TF-51D
  • Carrier operations with catapult launches and arrested landings
  • Professional-grade flight model and damage modeling
POPULAR AIRCRAFT MODULES:
  • F-16C Viper - Modern multirole fighter
  • F/A-18C Hornet - Navy's workhorse (HIGHLY recommended)
  • A-10C II Tank Killer - Close air support beast
  • F-14B Tomcat - Classic naval interceptor with Jester AI
  • AH-64D Apache - Full systems simulation helicopter
  • F-15E Strike Eagle - Dual-role strike fighter
  • MiG-21bis - Cold War interceptor challenge
TECHNICAL SPECS:
  • ENGINE: Eagle Dynamics proprietary
  • MIN REQUIREMENTS: GTX 1060 6GB / i5-8400 / 16GB RAM
  • RECOMMENDED: RTX 3070+ / i7-10700K / 32GB RAM
  • STORAGE: 120GB+ (grows with modules)
  • PERIPHERALS: HOTAS highly recommended, rudder pedals ideal
REALISM:
GRAPHICS:
BEGINNER FRIENDLY:
LEARNING CURVE:
Best for: Military aviation enthusiasts, those who want authentic combat procedures. Start with F/A-18C for best learning experience. Expect 30+ hours to become proficient with one aircraft.

Falcon BMS (Benchmark Sims)

FREE MOD
DEVELOPER: Benchmark Sims (community) RELEASE: Ongoing (latest: BMS 4.37) PLATFORMS: Windows (requires Falcon 4.0 base) PRICE: FREE (need Falcon 4.0 for ~$5) TYPE: F-16 focused combat simulation
OVERVIEW:

A community-developed overhaul of the classic Falcon 4.0. BMS is considered one of the most realistic F-16 simulators available, rivaling DCS in systems depth but with superior dynamic campaign engine. Free and actively developed.

KEY FEATURES:
  • Most detailed F-16C simulation available (block 50/52)
  • Legendary dynamic campaign (theater-wide war simulation)
  • Advanced avionics including Link-16, JHMCS, modern weapons
  • Realistic IFF, radar modes, and SEAD operations
  • Complete with AWACS, tankers, and ground-based command
  • Multiplayer tactical coordination
  • Regular updates from dedicated community
  • Steep but rewarding learning curve
REALISM:
GRAPHICS:
BEGINNER FRIENDLY:
Best for: Hardcore F-16 enthusiasts who want dynamic campaign. Graphics are dated but systems simulation is second to none.

Free & Open Source Simulators

FlightGear

100% FREE
DEVELOPER: FlightGear Community (open source) RELEASE: 1997 (latest: 2024.1) PLATFORMS: Windows, macOS, Linux PRICE: FREE (GPL licensed) TYPE: General aviation simulation
OVERVIEW:

FlightGear is a completely free, open-source flight simulator with a solid flight model and active development community. While graphics don't match commercial sims, it's perfect for learning aviation fundamentals without spending money.

KEY FEATURES:
  • 500+ aircraft available for download
  • Realistic flight dynamics using JSBSim engine
  • Global scenery (generated from real elevation data)
  • Full IFR capabilities with working navigation aids
  • Multi-monitor and multi-computer setup support
  • Active modding community
  • Educational use in schools and universities
  • Multiplayer flying capability
  • Can interface with real aviation hardware
REALISM:
GRAPHICS:
BEGINNER FRIENDLY:
COST:
Best for: Budget-conscious beginners, Linux users, students, tinkerers who enjoy customization. Great for learning Cessna 172 fundamentals.

War Thunder (Sim Mode)

FREE-TO-PLAY
DEVELOPER: Gaijin Entertainment PLATFORMS: PC, PlayStation, Xbox PRICE: Free (with premium options)
OVERVIEW:

War Thunder features three modes: Arcade (easy), Realistic (balanced), and Simulator (full sim). Simulator mode offers cockpit-only view, realistic flight models, and authentic combat. Massive selection of historical aircraft from all nations.

KEY FEATURES:
  • 2000+ vehicles including 800+ aircraft
  • WW1 biplanes to modern jets (F-16, MiG-29, etc.)
  • Simulator mode with full controls
  • Historical matchmaking
  • Combined arms battles (planes, tanks, ships)
  • Regular updates and new content
  • Active competitive scene
Simulator mode requires joystick. Free progression but can be grindy. Great middle ground between arcade and hardcore sims.

Classic & Retro Simulators

Microsoft Flight Simulator X (FSX)

LEGENDARY
DEVELOPER: Microsoft / Aces Game Studio RELEASE: 2006 PLATFORMS: Windows PC PRICE: $24.99 Steam Edition
OVERVIEW:

The legendary simulator that defined a generation. While outdated graphically, FSX still has the largest add-on library in history. Thousands of freeware aircraft and scenery available. Runs perfectly on modern low-end PCs.

WHY STILL RELEVANT:
  • Massive freeware add-on library (decades of content)
  • Runs on potato PCs
  • Study-level aircraft (PMDG, A2A, Carenado legacy)
  • Complete mission creator
  • Nostalgic value for veteran simmers
  • Still used in some training applications
  • Great for learning fundamentals
Despite being 19 years old, FSX remains playable and educational. Consider it if you have a weak PC or want access to massive freeware library.

Fly! / Fly! 2K

CULT CLASSIC
DEVELOPER: Terminal Reality RELEASE: 1999-2000
OVERVIEW:

Revolutionary for its time with accurate systems simulation and advanced weather. Featured detailed Cessna 182, Learjet 45, and Boeing 737. Beloved by retro sim enthusiasts but hard to find/run today.

Condor 2 Soaring Simulator

GLIDING

THE definitive glider/sailplane simulator. Used by real glider pilots for training. Features realistic thermal modeling, ridge lift, wave lift, and weather systems. Participate in online competitions and multiplayer soaring.

FS2004 (Flight Simulator 9)

RETRO
RELEASE: 2003 (100th anniversary of flight) STATUS: Abandonware / Legacy
OVERVIEW:

Predecessor to FSX, commemorating 100 years since Wright Brothers. Still beloved for its stability and massive add-on library. Simpler graphics but rock-solid performance. Many consider it more stable than FSX.

WHY SOME STILL USE IT:
  • More stable than FSX on some systems
  • Huge freeware library
  • Lower system requirements than FSX
  • Nostalgic value
  • Good for very old PCs

Quick Selection Guide

IF YOU WANT...
  • BEST GRAPHICS & IMMERSION: Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024
  • MOST REALISTIC FLIGHT MODEL: X-Plane 12
  • MILITARY COMBAT SIMULATION: DCS World
  • WW2 COMBAT: IL-2 Sturmovik
  • COMPLETELY FREE: FlightGear or DCS World (base)
  • MOBILE DEVICE: Infinite Flight
  • BEST PERFORMANCE: Aerofly FS 4
  • PROFESSIONAL TRAINING: X-Plane 12 or Prepar3D
  • FOR KIDS: Roblox PTFS or YSFlight
  • ARCADE ACTION: Ace Combat 7
  • GLIDER/SOARING: Condor 2
  • BUDGET PC: FlightGear or FSX
  • F-16 SPECIALIST: Falcon BMS
  • VR EXPERIENCE: MSFS 2024 or Aerofly FS 4
    • BEGINNER PATH:
    • START: MSFS 2024 with assists on OR FlightGear (free)
    • LEARN: Basic controls with Cessna 172
    • PROGRESS: Disable assists gradually
    • ADVANCE: Try IFR in X-Plane 12
    • OPTIONAL: Explore combat in DCS World
    HARDWARE RECOMMENDATIONS:
    • MINIMUM: Logitech Extreme 3D Pro joystick ($30-40)
    • BETTER: Thrustmaster T.16000M FCS HOTAS ($100-150)
    • SERIOUS: VKB Gladiator NXT + TWCS Throttle ($200-300)
    • YOKE OPTION: Honeycomb Alpha + Bravo ($400-500)
    • COMBAT: VKB or Virpil HOTAS + rudder pedals ($500+)
    • VR: Meta Quest 3 or Valve Index for best experience
    Don't buy expensive hardware until you've flown 20+ hours. Many people think they need a $500 setup but quit after a week. Start with a basic $40 joystick!
