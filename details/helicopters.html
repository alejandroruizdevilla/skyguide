Overview

Helicopter simulation offers a unique challenge compared to fixed-wing aircraft. The complex physics of rotary-wing flight, including collective, cyclic, and anti-torque pedal control, creates an incredibly rewarding experience for those who master it.

Whether you're interested in civilian operations like search and rescue, EMS, and sightseeing tours, or military combat helicopters, there's a simulator for every rotorhead.

Why Helicopters? Helicopter flying requires a different mindset - hovering, autorotations, and precise maneuvering in tight spaces offer challenges that fixed-wing pilots rarely encounter.

Top Helicopter Simulators

DCS World - Helicopters

Price: Free base + $40-80 per module

The gold standard for military helicopter simulation. Features incredibly detailed models of the Ka-50, Mi-8, Mi-24, UH-1H Huey, AH-64D Apache, and SA342 Gazelle. Each helicopter is a study-level simulation with full systems modeling.

X-Plane 12 Helicopters

Price: $59.99 + add-ons

X-Plane's blade element theory provides excellent helicopter flight modeling. Combined with payware add-ons like the DreamFoil Bell 407 or the upcoming HPG H145, it offers superb civilian helicopter simulation.

MSFS 2024 Helicopters

Price: $69.99+

Microsoft Flight Simulator 2024 introduces native helicopter support with improved flight dynamics. The stunning world scenery makes it perfect for VFR helicopter operations and sightseeing.

Take On Helicopters

Price: $19.99

Bohemia Interactive's dedicated helicopter sim. While older, it offers a unique career mode and realistic flight model. Great for those interested in civilian helicopter operations on a budget.

Airwolf (Rotorsim)

Price: Varies

Community-developed helicopter simulation projects offer free alternatives with varying levels of fidelity. Check forums for the latest releases.

Key Skills to Learn

Basic Maneuvers

  • Hovering - The fundamental helicopter skill
  • Hover taxi and air taxi
  • Normal takeoff and landing
  • Slope operations
  • Confined area operations

Advanced Techniques

  • Autorotation - Emergency engine-out procedure
  • Quick stops and rapid decelerations
  • Pinnacle and ridgeline operations
  • External load operations
  • Night vision goggle (NVG) flying

Recommended Hardware

Essential: A quality joystick with twist rudder or dedicated pedals is almost mandatory for helicopter simulation. The constant pedal inputs required make a twist grip uncomfortable for long sessions.

  • Rudder pedals (Thrustmaster TFRP, CH Pro Pedals, MFG Crosswind)
  • Joystick with good centering (VKB Gladiator, Virpil)
  • Throttle quadrant or collective lever
  • TrackIR or VR headset highly recommended
Pro Tip: Consider a dedicated collective controller for the most realistic experience. The Virpil Collective and Komodo Simulations Collective are popular choices among serious rotorheads.
