Overview

FlightGear is a completely free, open-source flight simulator that has been developed by a dedicated community for over 25 years. While it may not have the cutting-edge graphics of commercial simulators, it offers a solid flight model, global coverage, and the freedom to learn aviation fundamentals without spending a penny.

Perfect for students, aviation enthusiasts on a budget, or anyone who wants to try flight simulation before investing in commercial products. FlightGear proves that you don't need to spend hundreds of dollars to experience realistic flight.

Perfect for: Budget-conscious beginners, students learning aviation, Linux users, open-source enthusiasts, and anyone wanting to try flight simulation risk-free.

  • Cessna 172, PA-28, C130, 737, 777, and more

  • Quality varies but many excellent models

Global Scenery

  • Entire world covered
  • Free to download all scenery

  • Terrain based on real elevation data
  • Custom scenery available

Simulation Features

  • Decent flight physics
  • Full IFR capabilities with working navaids
  • Real weather integration
  • Multiplayer support
  • Air traffic control simulation

Open Source Benefits

Pros & Cons

Strengths

  • Completely free - no cost whatsoever
  • Runs on Linux, Mac, and Windows
  • 500+ aircraft available
  • Global scenery coverage
  • Good for learning IFR procedures
  • Active community
  • No DRM or licensing restrictions
  • Educational and learning-friendly

Limitations

  • Graphics dated compared to commercial sims
  • Setup can be confusing for beginners
  • Aircraft quality varies significantly
  • Interface not as polished
  • Performance optimization needed
  • Smaller active user base
  • Steeper learning curve for setup

Getting Started

Installation

  • Download from flightgear.org
  • Simple installer for all platforms
  • Download additional aircraft and scenery as needed
  • Completely free - no accounts or purchases

First Flight

  • Start with Cessna 172p (default aircraft)
  • Choose a familiar airport
  • Work through built-in tutorials
  • Join FlightGear multiplayer for help

Best Uses

  • Learning basic aviation concepts
