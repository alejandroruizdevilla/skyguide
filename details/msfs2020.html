Overview

Released in August 2020, Microsoft Flight Simulator 2020 revolutionized the flight simulation genre with its groundbreaking use of cloud technology and satellite imagery. This is the simulator that proved photorealistic flight simulation was possible, bringing unprecedented visual fidelity to home computers.

While MSFS 2024 has since been released, MSFS 2020 remains an excellent choice with a mature ecosystem, lower system requirements, and years of community development. It offers the same stunning global scenery and continues to receive updates, making it a fantastic value proposition especially for those on mid-range hardware.

Perfect for: Anyone who wants the MSFS experience with better performance and stability. Ideal if you have a mid-range PC or prefer a mature platform with extensive third-party support and proven reliability.

Key Features

Proven Technology

  • Entire Earth with photorealistic satellite imagery (Bing Maps integration)
  • 37,000+ hand-crafted airports
  • Real-time weather with live METAR integration
  • Volumetric clouds and atmospheric scattering
  • Better optimized than MSFS 2024 for current hardware

Aircraft Library

  • 30+ included aircraft (Standard edition) up to 35+ (Premium Deluxe)
  • Iconic aircraft: Cessna 172, Diamond DA62, Boeing 787, Airbus A320neo
  • Extensive third-party aircraft library (PMDG 737, FenixSim A320, etc.)
  • Years of add-on development and refinement
  • Full range from ultralights to jumbo jets

Mature Ecosystem

  • Massive marketplace with thousands of add-ons
  • Stable platform with known workarounds
  • Extensive community documentation and tutorials
  • Compatible with all major flight sim hardware
  • Active modding community with free content

Simulation Features

  • Full VR support (all major headsets)
  • Multiplayer with shared cockpit
  • Flight training lessons included
  • Bush trips and landing challenges
  • Real-world air traffic integration

Pros & Cons

Strengths

  • Better optimized and more stable than MSFS 2024
  • Massive library of proven third-party aircraft and scenery
  • Lower system requirements for similar visual quality
  • Four years of community knowledge and tutorials
  • Extensive marketplace with competitive pricing
  • Available on Game Pass (incredible value)
  • Proven reliability for serious simulation
  • Active multiplayer and VATSIM community
  • All major add-on developers support it

Limitations

  • No career mode (unlike MSFS 2024)
  • Slightly older graphics engine
  • Some flight model improvements only in 2024
  • Still requires internet connection for streaming
  • Large storage footprint
  • Some aircraft systems less detailed in default planes

Getting Started

For Beginners

Start with the comprehensive Flight Training section. The tutorials cover everything from basic controls to IFR procedures. Begin with the Cessna 152 with all assists enabled. Practice at your local airport with clear weather.

Recommended First Flight

  • Aircraft: Cessna 172 Skyhawk (G1000)
  • Location: Start at your nearest airport or KSEA (Seattle)
  • Weather: Live weather or clear preset
  • Time: Morning or afternoon for best lighting
  • Activity: Pattern work or local sightseeing

Essential Add-ons to Consider

  • PMDG 737-700/800 (study-level Boeing airliner)
  • FenixSim A320 (best Airbus simulation)
  • Orbx regions for enhanced scenery
  • REX Weather Force for weather enhancement
  • A2A Comanche for GA perfection

Hardware Recommendations

  • Entry Joystick: Thrustmaster T.16000M
  • Best Yoke: Honeycomb Alpha Flight Controls
  • Throttle Quadrant: Honeycomb Bravo Throttle
  • Rudder Pedals: Thrustmaster TFRP
  • VR: Meta Quest 3 or HP Reverb G2

MSFS 2020 vs MSFS 2024

Choose MSFS 2020 if:

  • You have a mid-range PC (RTX 3060, RX 6700 level)
  • You want proven stability and reliability
  • You prefer mature add-on ecosystem
  • You want better value (often on sale, Game Pass)
  • You don't need the career mode

Choose MSFS 2024 if:

  • You have high-end hardware (RTX 4070+)
  • You want the latest graphics features
  • Career mode appeals to you
  • You want cutting-edge technology
  • You're willing to accept early adopter issues
