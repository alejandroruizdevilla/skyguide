Overview

Prepar3D (pronounced "prepared") is a professional-grade flight simulation platform developed by Lockheed Martin, primarily designed for training, education, and research. Built on the legacy of Microsoft Flight Simulator, it has evolved into a sophisticated platform used by airlines, military organizations, and serious simulation enthusiasts.

Unlike consumer-focused simulators, Prepar3D prioritizes accuracy, stability, and professional features over accessibility. It's the platform of choice for commercial pilot training programs, corporate flight departments, and simulation professionals who need reliable, repeatable results for certification and training purposes.

Perfect for: Professional pilots, flight schools, serious simulation enthusiasts with legacy FSX add-ons, and anyone requiring professional-grade simulation features. Academic license offers excellent value for students.

Getting Started

Licensing

Prepar3D has specific license types. Academic ($59.95) is for educational use and hobbyists. Professional ($199.95) is for commercial training. Ensure you select the appropriate license. Academic license is NOT for commercial use.

For New Users

Prepar3D assumes aviation knowledge. Start with default aircraft and build your way up. The interface is similar to FSX if you're familiar with it. Study the comprehensive documentation before diving in.

Essential Add-ons

  • PMDG 737/777 (study-level airliners)
  • Majestic Q400 (regional turboprop)
  • ORBX scenery packages
  • Active Sky for weather
  • REX Skyforce for textures
  • GSX for ground services

Recommended First Flight

  • Aircraft: Default Boeing 737-800
  • Location: KLAX Los Angeles or familiar airport
  • Weather: Clear preset
  • Activity: IFR flight with autopilot
  • Focus: Systems familiarization

For Professional Training

  • Review SDK documentation
  • Set up scenario-based training
  • Configure instructor station if applicable
  • Utilize recording and playback features
  • Integrate with professional hardware

Who Should Choose Prepar3D?

Professional Pilots

If you're a commercial pilot looking to practice procedures at home, Prepar3D offers the stability and accuracy needed for serious training.

Flight Schools

Training organizations benefit from the professional SDK, multi-station support, and proven reliability for curriculum development.

FSX Veterans

If you have extensive FSX add-ons, Prepar3D lets you continue using them in a modern 64-bit platform with better graphics and stability.

Serious Simmers

Enthusiasts who value simulation depth over graphics and want access to proven professional-grade aircraft like PMDG will appreciate Prepar3D.
