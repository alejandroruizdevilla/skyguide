Overview

X-Plane 12 is widely regarded as the most physically accurate flight simulator available to consumers. Unlike other simulators that use lookup tables and approximations, X-Plane calculates aerodynamic forces in real-time using blade element theory, resulting in incredibly realistic flight behavior across all flight regimes.

This is the simulator of choice for serious aviation enthusiasts, professional pilots, and flight schools. FAA-certified versions of X-Plane are used for actual pilot training and logging flight hours. If you want to truly understand how aircraft fly and experience authentic flight dynamics, X-Plane 12 is unmatched.

Perfect for: Serious simulation enthusiasts, professional pilots, flight training, IFR practice, and anyone who values accurate flight physics over visual spectacle. The go-to choice for realistic helicopter simulation.

Getting Started

For Beginners

X-Plane 12 assumes some aviation knowledge. Start with the Cessna 172 and work through basic maneuvers in clear weather. Use the flight school mode and read the comprehensive manual. Consider pairing with real-world flight training materials.

Recommended First Flight

  • Aircraft: Cessna 172SP (default)
  • Location: KBOS Boston Logan or your local airport
  • Weather: Clear preset weather
  • Activity: Pattern work and touch-and-goes
  • Focus: Feel the aircraft's response and behavior

Must-Have Add-ons

  • ToLiss A321 (best Airbus for X-Plane)
  • IXEG 737 Classic (study-level Boeing)
  • Zibo 737-800 (free, incredible quality)
  • FlightFactor A320 Ultimate
  • Orbx TrueEarth regions for scenery
  • XEnviro or Active Sky for weather

For Helicopter Enthusiasts

  • Start with the included Bell 407
  • Practice hovering in calm conditions
  • Master forward flight transitions
  • Progress to the more complex S-76
  • Consider DreamFoil Creations helicopters

Hardware Recommendations

  • Joystick: Thrustmaster T.16000M or VKB Gladiator
  • Yoke: Honeycomb Alpha or CH Products Eclipse
  • Rudder Pedals: Thrustmaster TFRP or VKB T-Rudders
  • For Helicopters: Virpil or VKB collective highly recommended
